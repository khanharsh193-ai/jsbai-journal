Aims & Scope

The Journal of Sustainable Biosciences and Agricultural Innovation (JSBAI) is a double-blind peer-reviewed, open access quarterly journal committed to disseminating high-quality original research, systematic reviews, rapid communications, and methodological advances at the intersection of biological sciences and agricultural systems.

Founded in 2018, JSBAI operates under BioAgri Academic Publishers Pvt. Ltd. and maintains an editorial commitment to scientific rigour, reproducibility, and practical relevance to challenges facing Indian and global agriculture. The journal gives special priority to research addressing food security, climate adaptation, and sustainable intensification.

Article Types Accepted
  • Original Research Articles — Novel experimental findings with full Methods, Results, and Discussion (5,000–8,000 words)
  • Review Articles — Comprehensive critical reviews of a defined topic with systematic literature coverage (6,000–12,000 words)
  • Short Communications — Concise reports of significant preliminary findings (1,500–3,000 words)
  • Rapid Communications — Urgent findings of exceptional importance, expedited review (max 2,500 words)
  • Methodology Papers — Novel techniques, protocols, or computational tools with validation data
  • Case Studies — Well-documented field or laboratory case studies with broader implications
  • Letters to the Editor — Brief scientific correspondence responding to published articles (max 600 words)

Manuscript Preparation
  • Prepare in Microsoft Word (.docx) using Times New Roman 12pt, double-spaced, A4 page size with 2.5 cm margins on all sides.
  • Structure: Title Page → Abstract → Keywords → Introduction → Materials & Methods → Results → Discussion → Conclusions → Acknowledgements → References → Figure Legends → Tables → Figures.
  • The Title Page must contain: full title, all authors' names, affiliations, corresponding author's email, ORCID iDs, and a running title of no more than 60 characters.
  • For blind review, remove all identifying information from the main manuscript file. Author details go on the title page only.
  • Word limits: Original Research 5,000–8,000 words; Review Articles 6,000–12,000 words; Short Communications 1,500–3,000 words; Rapid Communications max 2,500 words. Word count excludes abstract, references, and figure legends.
Abstract & Keywords
  • Structured abstract of max 300 words with labelled sections: Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions.
  • For Review Articles: Background, Scope, Key Findings, Conclusions.
  • Provide 4–8 keywords not appearing in the title, ordered from most to least specific.
Citation & Reference Style
  • JSBAI follows APA 7th Edition throughout.
  • In-text: (Author, Year); (Author & Author, Year); (Author et al., Year) for three or more authors.
  • References listed alphabetically at the end. DOIs must be included for all journal articles where available, formatted as hyperlinks.
  • Self-citation should not exceed 15% of total references.
  • Personal communications, unpublished data, and preprints may be cited with appropriate qualification but do not count toward the reference list minimum.
Figures, Tables & Data
  • Figures: minimum 300 DPI (600 DPI for micrographs); TIFF or high-resolution JPEG preferred; label axes clearly.
  • Colour figures are published online at no charge; print colour may attract a supplement fee.
  • Tables must be editable (Word format), not embedded as images. Each table requires a title and footnotes explaining all abbreviations.
  • Raw datasets should be deposited in a public repository (Zenodo, Figshare, DRYAD) and linked with a persistent identifier in the manuscript.
  • Statistical analyses must specify software used, version, and all relevant parameters.
Ethics & Declaration Requirements
  • Research involving human participants requires IRB/Ethics Committee approval — state approval number in Methods.
  • Animal studies must comply with the Committee for the Purpose of Control and Supervision of Experiments on Animals (CPCSEA) guidelines.
  • All authors must disclose conflicts of interest (or state "None declared").
  • Funding sources and grant numbers must be declared in the Acknowledgements.
  • For multi-author papers, an Author Contributions statement is mandatory using CRediT taxonomy.

Article Processing Charges

JSBAI is a fully open access journal — all published articles are immediately and permanently available free of charge to all readers worldwide. To cover editorial and production costs, Article Processing Charges (APCs) apply in most cases. However, JSBAI maintains a strong waiver policy for researchers from India and low-income countries.

Waiver Policy

Authors from least-developed countries (LDCs) and those demonstrating genuine financial hardship may apply for a full or partial waiver. Waiver requests should be submitted at the time of manuscript submission by contacting the editorial office. Waivers are granted at editorial discretion and do not influence the peer review process in any way.

Payment Methods

APCs are invoiced after formal acceptance. Payment can be made by bank transfer (NEFT/RTGS for Indian authors), credit/debit card via secured payment gateway, or institutional purchase order. APCs are non-refundable once production has commenced.

Reviewer Guidelines

Role of Peer Reviewers

Peer reviewers play a critical role in maintaining the scientific integrity and quality of JSBAI. We appreciate the significant time commitment involved and acknowledge all reviewers annually in our published Reviewer Acknowledgement.

Review Criteria

  • Originality: Does the work advance the field? Is it genuinely novel?
  • Scientific rigour: Are methods appropriate, sound, and reproducible?
  • Data quality: Are results clearly presented and statistically valid?
  • Interpretation: Are conclusions supported by the data presented?
  • References: Is relevant literature cited appropriately and completely?
  • Ethics: Does the study comply with applicable ethical standards?
  • Clarity: Is the manuscript clearly written and well-structured?

Timelines & Process

  • Reviewers are expected to confirm availability within 3 working days of invitation.
  • Reviews should be completed within 4 weeks of accepting the assignment.
  • If unable to complete within the deadline, notify the editorial office immediately.
  • Reviews must be confidential — manuscripts should not be shared or discussed with others.

Conflicts of Interest

Reviewers must decline assignments where a conflict of interest exists — including personal relationships, institutional rivalry, or any financial interest in the research. When in doubt, contact the editorial office.

Publication Ethics

JSBAI is a signatory to the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) guidelines. All parties — authors, reviewers, and editors — are expected to adhere to the highest ethical standards in scientific publishing.

Authors' Responsibilities

  • All data presented must be accurate, complete, and honestly reported. Fabrication, falsification, or selective reporting of data constitutes research misconduct.
  • Simultaneous submission to multiple journals is prohibited.
  • All persons who made substantial intellectual contributions to the work must be listed as authors (ICMJE criteria). Honorary authorship is not accepted.
  • Any prior publication of the data (including preprints) must be disclosed at submission.

Plagiarism Policy

All submissions are screened using iThenticate on receipt. Manuscripts with a similarity index exceeding 15% (excluding reference list and standard phrases) will be returned to the authors without entering peer review. Manuscripts found to contain plagiarised content after publication will be retracted.

Post-Publication Corrections

JSBAI operates a transparent corrections policy. Authors who identify errors in their published work should contact the editorial office immediately. Corrections, expressions of concern, and retractions are published openly and linked to the original article.

Open Access Policy

JSBAI is a fully Gold Open Access journal. All articles are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) licence, which permits unrestricted use, distribution, and reproduction in any medium, provided the original work is properly cited.

JSBAI is listed in the Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ) and complies with the DOAJ's criteria for open access transparency and best practice. There are no embargo periods; all content is immediately open access upon publication.

Self-Archiving / Green Open Access

Authors are permitted to self-archive the accepted manuscript (post-peer-review, pre-typeset version) in their institutional repository or a subject repository (e.g., PubMed Central, AgriXiv) immediately upon acceptance. The final published PDF may also be self-archived immediately with appropriate citation.

Plagiarism Policy

Screening Process

Every manuscript submitted to JSBAI is automatically screened for text similarity using iThenticate powered by CrossCheck. Screening occurs before the manuscript enters editorial evaluation.

Thresholds & Actions

  • Below 15%: Manuscript proceeds to editorial evaluation.
  • 15–25%: Editorial review of matched sections — returned for revision if significant overlap is identified.
  • Above 25%: Returned to authors without review. Resubmission may be considered after substantial revision.
  • Verbatim copying without attribution: Immediate rejection; authors may be barred from future submissions.

Self-Plagiarism

Reuse of the authors' own previously published text without attribution constitutes self-plagiarism and is treated seriously. Authors must cite and clearly distinguish any prior work from new contributions.

Copyright & Licensing

Licence

All articles published in JSBAI are licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) licence. This allows readers to copy, distribute, transmit, and adapt the work for any purpose, provided appropriate credit is given, a link to the licence is provided, and any changes made are indicated.

Copyright Retention

Authors retain copyright to their work. By submitting to JSBAI, authors grant the journal a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free licence to publish and distribute the article in all formats, including print, electronic, and any future media. Authors may use their published article for educational and research purposes without restriction.

Third-Party Content

Authors are responsible for obtaining permission to reproduce any third-party material (figures, tables, data) included in their manuscript. Written permission must be provided to the editorial office before publication.

Frequently Asked Questions
How long does peer review take? +
Initial editorial screening takes 5–7 working days. Full double-blind peer review typically takes 6–10 weeks from the date the manuscript is sent to reviewers. Rapid Communications are reviewed within 3 weeks. Total time from submission to first decision averages 8–10 weeks.
Is there a publication charge for Indian authors? +
No. Article Processing Charges are completely waived for corresponding authors affiliated with any recognised Indian institution — including all universities, IITs, IISc, ICAR institutes, CSIR labs, NIT, and state agricultural universities. No payment is required at any stage of submission or publication.
Can I submit a manuscript that has been posted as a preprint? +
Yes. JSBAI accepts manuscripts that have been posted on preprint servers (e.g., bioRxiv, AgriXiv). Authors must disclose the preprint at submission and include the DOI or URL of the preprint. The preprint should not be updated to the final published version without acknowledgement of the journal.
What is the acceptance rate? +
JSBAI maintains an acceptance rate of approximately 28%. All manuscripts undergo rigorous double-blind peer review by at least two independent experts. The main reasons for rejection include insufficient novelty, methodological weaknesses, and poor fit with the journal's scope.
Can I suggest reviewers? +
Yes, you may suggest up to 3 preferred reviewers and specify up to 2 reviewers you wish to be excluded. All suggestions are considered at editorial discretion. You may not suggest reviewers from your own institution or with known conflicts of interest.
How do I track my submission? +
Use the "Track Submission" feature under the "For Authors" menu. Enter your Reference ID (e.g., JSBAI-2025-XXXX) and the email address used during submission. Your Reference ID is included in the automated confirmation email you received when you submitted.
What licence are articles published under? +
All articles are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) licence. Authors retain copyright. The licence allows anyone to share and adapt the work for any purpose as long as appropriate credit is given.
How do I withdraw my manuscript? +
To withdraw a manuscript under review, contact the editorial office at editor.jsbai@bioagriacad.in quoting your Reference ID. Withdrawal is free before peer review commences. Withdrawal after review has begun requires a written explanation and may attract an administrative fee of USD 30 to compensate reviewers for their time.
